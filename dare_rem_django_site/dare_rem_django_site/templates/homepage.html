Big Data-scale Biocomputing Research

A group of researchers from the center for computation and technology, LSU, is working closely with investigators from biological sciences, biomedical diseases, and computer science at LSU.

What We Do?

We investigate, develop, apply, and distribute BigData discovery and outcomes in a collaborative manner for life science projects.

What We Envision?

Cyberinfrastructure integrating software frameworks and scientific activities is a viable approach to deal with ongoing large-scale biological computing and data analytics research.

Focus Areas

The following Big Data applications are actively investigated along with DARE-based gateway projects by invesetigators in Advanced High-performance and Distributed Biocomputing (AHDB) Lab at CCT

Science Gateway for Life Science BigData Research

Science gateways are effective means for collaborative research activities with the capability to utilize large scale High-performance computing (HPC) resources and databases located in many different places.

BigData Genomics

Our research interest is to develop novel strategies for large scale bigdata genomics projects.

Next Generation Sequencing Analytics

We are currently engaged in genelab NGS sequencing core, BIOMMED, School of Vet. Medicine, LSU and NIH COBRE as a bioinformatics support team
